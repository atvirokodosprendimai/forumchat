Who we are

Commons is self-hosted community software that combines realtime chat, forum discussions, video rooms and an optional AI assistant. The community you are signing in to runs on a server operated by its administrators (“the operator”). This policy explains what data the Commons software handles on the operator’s behalf, and how the Google sign-in integration works.

Because Commons is open source (AGPL-3.0) and self-hosted, your community’s data lives on infrastructure the operator controls — not on a shared platform owned by us. References to “we”, “us” and “the service” below mean the operator of this Commons instance.

Information we collect

We collect only what is needed to run a community space. There is no advertising profile, no tracking pixel, and no data broker in the middle.

Account information

  • Email address — used to identify your account, send sign-in and verification links, and (if enabled) notify you.
  • Display name — shown next to your messages and on your profile.
  • Password — only for accounts that set one, and stored solely as a salted bcrypt hash. Accounts that use Google or a magic link never have a usable password on file.

Content you create

  • Messages, threads, posts, notes and comments you write in the community.
  • Files you upload (images, documents, and similar).

By design, this content is shared with the other members of your community — Commons is a public-to-the-community space, not a private messaging app.

Technical information

  • Session data — an essential cookie that keeps you signed in (see Cookies).
  • Server logs — standard records such as IP address, browser user-agent and timestamps, retained for security, debugging and abuse prevention.

Google sign-in data

When you choose “Continue with Google”, Google asks for your consent and then shares a limited set of profile details with us. We request only these scopes:

  • openid — a stable, anonymous identifier for your Google account.
  • email — your email address and whether Google has verified it.
  • profile — your basic public profile: name and profile picture.

In short, all we receive is your email address and your basic public profile — your name, and your profile picture if you have one — together with the Google account identifier that links your future sign-ins to the same account. Nothing else.

We do not request — and therefore cannot read — your Gmail, Google Drive, Calendar, Contacts, or any other Google service data. Sign-in is the only purpose.

Limited use of Google user data

Our use of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements. Specifically, data obtained through Google sign-in is used only to authenticate you and to populate your member profile (name and avatar). We do not sell it, do not use it for advertising, and do not transfer it to third parties except as needed to provide the sign-in feature or where required by law.

How we use information

  • To run your account — create and authenticate it, recognise you on return visits, and link a Google identity to an existing account with the same verified email.
  • To display your presence — show your name and avatar beside your contributions.
  • To deliver the service — store and show the chat, threads, posts and files you and other members create.
  • To keep things working and safe — diagnose problems, prevent abuse, and honour bans or moderation decisions.
  • To communicate — send verification, sign-in and (opt-in) notification messages.

We do not use your information for advertising, profiling, or automated decisions with legal effects.

Why we are allowed to (legal bases)

Where data-protection law such as the GDPR applies, we rely on:

  • Contract — to give you the account and community features you signed up for.
  • Legitimate interests — to keep the service secure, reliable and free of abuse.
  • Consent — for optional things you switch on, such as push notifications. You can withdraw consent at any time.
  • Legal obligation — where we must retain or disclose data to comply with the law.

Storage & security

  • Self-hosted by design. Everything — every account, message, thread, and file — lives in a single database and an uploads folder on the operator’s own server. No telemetry phones home and no analytics broker sits in the middle.
  • Passwords are hashed with bcrypt; they are never stored or logged in plain text.
  • Secrets are encrypted at rest (AES-GCM) where the deployment stores integration credentials such as OAuth client secrets.
  • Your discussions stay yours. The optional AI assistant runs on models the operator hosts; by default no third-party AI service reads your community’s content.
  • Transport security. When served over HTTPS, traffic between your browser and the server is encrypted, and session cookies are marked Secure in production.

No system is perfectly secure, but data is kept on infrastructure the operator controls and is exposed to as few parties as possible.

Sharing & disclosure

We do not sell your personal data, and there are no advertising networks involved. Information is disclosed only:

  • Within your community — the content you post is visible to other members, as that is the point of the space.
  • To essential providers — for example an email relay to deliver sign-in messages, or Google for the sign-in step itself. These handle data only to provide their function.
  • When legally required — to comply with a valid legal request, or to protect the rights, safety and property of the community and its members.

Retention & deletion

We keep your information for as long as your account is active, then as described here:

  • Delete your account. You can delete your account from your profile. Your email is released, login is disabled, and personal records are removed; content you shared with the community is anonymised and reattributed to a “deleted user” so other members’ conversations stay intact.
  • Export your data. Community owners can export the community’s data as a downloadable archive.
  • Community deletion. An operator can delete an entire community, which irreversibly erases its messages, threads, files and member records.
  • Logs are retained only as long as useful for security and debugging, then rotated out.

Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, export, restrict, or delete your personal data, and to object to certain processing. Commons gives you direct tools for most of this — editing your profile, exporting community data, and deleting your account — and you can contact us for anything else. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data-protection authority.

Cookies

Commons uses only the cookies it needs to function — there are no advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

forumchat_session
Essential. Keeps you signed in. HttpOnly, SameSite=Lax, and Secure in production.
OAuth state cookie
Short-lived. Set only during a Google sign-in to protect the round-trip against forgery, then discarded.

Children

Commons is not directed at children. The service is intended for users who are old enough to consent to data processing under their local law (generally 13, or 16 in parts of the EU). Operators may set their own age requirements for a community; if you believe a child has provided personal data, contact us and we will remove it.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the software or the law evolves. When we do, we revise the “Last updated” date above, and we announce material changes within the community. Continued use after a change means you accept the updated policy.
